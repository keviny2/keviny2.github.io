Education

I am currently finishing my MSc. in Bioinformatics at the University of British Columbia (UBC), working at the BC Cancer Agency under the supervision of Dr. Andrew Roth. I received my BSc. in Computer Science and Statistics with distinction, also at UBC in the Winter of 2020.

Research

As part of my MSc. thesis, I am developing a Bayesian network named LiquidBayes to infer clonal prevalences in cancer patients. LiquidBayes integrates biological information from previously inferred copy number (CN) profiles with cell free DNA (cfDNA) in liquid biopsies to monitor treatment progression. Incidentally, I constructed various bioinformatics pipelines to perform GC/mappability bias correction in read count data and somatic variant calling. LiquidBayes is implemented in Python using the Pyro Probabilistic Programming Language (PPL). My research is partly funded by the Canadian Institutes of Health Research (CIHR) CGS+M scholarship.
